Workstation · Engine · Worlds

AtlasForge is a simulation-first stack for games, tools, and docs.

The ecosystem links a multi-panel workstation, a routing brain, and living worlds into one coherent interface. AtlasStudio handles the work, Atlas-V handles the logic, and worlds like Octopus in Action and S/K/B supply the stories and sound.

Use the stack to prototype workflows, test ideas, and sketch how your own projects could flow through a real workstation and engine later.

Sheets → Workstation

OIA BuildLogs define how work feels inside AtlasStudio.

Feature queues, decisions, and snapshots are designed to mirror the way your build spreadsheets already work—just with a better surface.

Feature queues

Items from BuildLogs and project sheets enter the workstation as feature cards. Each card keeps its category, tags, and notes so nothing is lost in translation.

v1.0.0 Initial queues wired from OIA sheets.

Decisions

Yes, No, Later, and Expand become first-class actions. Every choice updates the card state and can drive follow-ups, risk flags, or future snapshots.

v1.0.1 Decision patterns aligned with BuildLog markers.

Snapshots

Each pass can be recorded as a snapshot: which features moved, which stalled, and which resurfaced. The language is borrowed from versioned builds in your spreadsheets.

v1.0.3 Snapshot strip echoes BuildLog _SNAPSHOT_* tabs.
