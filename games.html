Worlds & projects

Worlds plug into AtlasForge like clients on the same stack.

Each project uses the AtlasStudio + Atlas-V language in its own way: harbor cities, audio labs, and future experiments all share the same idea of tasks, routes, and builds.

This page collects current and planned worlds in one place.

Flagship world

Octopus in Action · harbor city sim

A compact coastal city with dock traffic, night markets, hillside roads, and neighborhoods that react to how well you keep everything moving.

What it is

Part management game, part neighborhood simulator. You orchestrate routes, patrols, deliveries, and events across districts, shifts, and changing conditions.

The city’s requests flow through a mailbox and become tasks inside AtlasStudio, where decisions shape the next rounds.

Visit OIA world page

How it uses the stack

Atlas-V handles routing, risk, and timing for OIA’s systems. Navigator decides which tasks surface, Guard enforces limits, Memory keeps decision history, and FlowSim runs city timelines.

Audio lab

S/K/B · soundtracks and stems

S/K/B Audio Lab builds sound palettes that respond to world state: quiet harbor mornings, busy markets, festival nights, and wind-soaked hills.

What it is

A workshop for ambient beds, loops, stems, and alternate cuts. Each pack is tuned to a specific mood and time slice in the worlds.

Visit S/K/B Audio Lab

How it plugs in

World state and session context can select which pack plays. Shifts in AtlasStudio sessions and Atlas-V scenarios can drive which stems are “active” at any moment.
