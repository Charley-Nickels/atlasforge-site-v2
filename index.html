Simulation-first demos · Front-end only

AtlasForge behaves like a real stack, but it’s all smoke and JavaScript.

This site is a controlled illusion: a workstation shell, an engine fiction, and world pages that all act like a production ecosystem—without any servers, uploads, or external AI calls.

AtlasStudio is the UI shell. Atlas-V is the routing brain. Octopus in Action is the flagship world. S/K/B is the audio lab that glues the vibes together.

Everything here runs in your browser: scripted workflows, mock decisions, and fake Copilot messages. No file access, no uploads, no hidden services.

Current focus

We’re wiring the AtlasStudio demo to mirror your OIA BuildLog behavior: feature queues, Yes/No/Later/Expand decisions, and pretend snapshots between v1.0.0 and v1.0.3.

The goal is to make the fake flows feel real enough that you can design processes inside them before any backend exists.

What this actually is

AtlasForge is a sandbox for believable prototypes, not a live product.

Every page is honest about what’s real today: guided workflows, blueprints, and front-end only demos that simulate how a future stack could behave.

BuildLog → UI translation

How the OIA spreadsheets secretly drive the workstation demo.

AtlasStudio’s fake flows are modeled after your OIA BuildLogs: feature rows with decisions, notes, and snapshots between versions.

Feature queues

Features from different projects (OIA, Atlas-V, S/K/B) are surfaced as cards in the left column of the workstation demo. The selection logic is scripted, but feels like a pulled-from-spreadsheet queue.

v1.0.0 Initial brainstorm deck wired to cards.

Decisions: Yes / No / Later / Expand

The center panel shows one feature at a time with decision buttons. Clicking them updates local state and triggers different fake Copilot reactions—mirroring how your spreadsheet decisions and notes work.

v1.0.1 Decisions log out to the on-screen console.

Snapshots & pretend diffs

The right panel summarises a “current build” with highlights like “2 features moved to Patch” or “Later items returned”. All of this is generated on the fly from local arrays.

v1.0.3 Snapshot strip hints at BuildLog `_SNAPSHOT_*` tabs.

Try the workstation demo with the right expectations.

The AtlasStudio demo is intentionally fake. It exists to help you design flows, not to process real data. If you want to turn this into a real product later, the same UI can sit on top of an actual backend.
