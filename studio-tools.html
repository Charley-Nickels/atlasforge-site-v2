The layout

The three-panel system gives projects a predictable home.

Columns are designed around flow: navigate on the left, act in the center, review on the right.

Left panel: navigation

Feature queues, tags, task sets, worlds, rounds, and quick-jumps. Structured so you can move between domains without losing state.

Center panel: actions

Features appear as cards. Each card carries its details, notes, and decision actions: Yes, No, Later, Expand.

Right panel: state & snapshots

The right column reflects the current build: flags, risks, recent decisions, and version-style snapshots tied to the active session.

Interaction

A consistent decision language across the entire stack.

AtlasStudio standardizes decision-making across all worlds and tools.

Yes

Moves the feature forward or marks it approved. Often reveals the next step or follow-up category.

No

Rejects or removes the feature from active rounds. May trigger cleanup or archive behaviors.

Later

Defers the feature into a re-prompts queue. Items placed in Later always return in future passes.

Expand

Generates follow-up questions or sub-features. Designed for discovery and refinement cycles.

Versioning

Snapshots capture how each pass changes the project.

AtlasStudio uses snapshot strips to represent movement between passes: features approved, dropped, deferred, or expanded.

Build snapshots

Each session can generate a snapshot—what moved, what stalled, and which chains or tasks changed state. Helps track momentum and bottlenecks.

Sheet-compatible

Snapshots echo the structure used in OIA’s versioned BuildLogs (v1.0.0 → v1.0.3), making it easy to export or compare notes later.
