Octopus in Action Cozy harbor sim · Gentle civic chaos

A small harbor, a gentleman octopus, and a town full of decisions.

Octopus in Action is a simulation about running a harbor-town where every task, schedule, and civic choice nudges the story. You’re working with (and occasionally against) Mayor Octavius — a polite octopus in a top hat — to keep the town alive.

Back to games & worlds See the tools shaping the harbor

The game is currently in an early design & prototype phase. This page captures the tone, systems, and role it plays inside the AtlasForge stack.

The pitch

Cozy, systemic, quietly weird.

OIA treats work, civic life, and relationships as first-class gameplay. It’s not about saving the world in one big moment — it’s about thousands of small, reversible decisions.

Core loop

Your day starts at the mailbox and ends with how the town feels at night.

  • Check mailbox for civic tasks and requests
  • Visit city hall, docks, market, and homes
  • See how people’s routines drift over time

Atmosphere

Gentle pacing, warm colors, and small dramas instead of constant crisis.

  • Morning fog and quiet streets
  • Midday bustle at docks and markets
  • Soft, reflective nights on the pier

Mayor & town

Mayor Octavius & the harbor citizens.

The town is small enough that everyone matters. Octavius isn’t a perfect leader — he’s trying, learning, and occasionally messing up in public, just like you.

  • Mayor Octavius: kind, a little dramatic, deeply invested in keeping things fair.
  • Dock workers & shop owners: they care more about whether they can pay rent than grand speeches.
  • Families & night workers: live on the other side of your decisions about noise, schedules, and resources.

Types of choices

The game focuses on choices that feel small but compound over time:

  • Which boats get dawn access to the docks?
  • Who gets warned vs. fined for small infractions?
  • How strict should curfews and noise rules be?

Under the hood

OIA as a testbed for Atlas-V.

Every mailbox task, city hall decision, and NPC schedule is an excuse to test how Atlas-V handles routing, memory, and guard rails.

  • Atlas-V decides which tasks surface first and why.
  • Memory tracks what the town “remembers” you doing.
  • Guard prevents no-win spirals or permanent soft locks.
  • FlowSim explores alternate futures behind the scenes.

Tools behind the game

OIA is being designed alongside the AtlasStudio workstation:

  • Brainstorm panel for new loops and systems
  • UAT checklists for tasks and schedules
  • Patch notes that talk like the game, not a changelog
