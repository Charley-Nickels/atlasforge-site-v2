Engine

Atlas-V: a simulation & automation core.

Atlas-V is our internal term for the logic and content engine that coordinates worlds and workflows. It’s opinionated enough to be useful, and flexible enough to sit beside your existing stack.

  • Structured logic: build systems out of nodes, routes, and patches instead of one-off scripts.
  • Content-aware: designed to sit near your content (docs, sprites, clips, knowledge) and reason about how it should be used.
  • Explainable behavior: the goal is not black-box magic, but clear timelines and diffs for what changed and why.
  • Simulation-first: think “what happens if…” with safe sandboxes before you roll changes into a live world or process.

Workstation

AtlasStudio: a focused place to work with AI, not fight it.

AtlasStudio is our internal workstation for building games, tools, and documentation with Atlas-V in the loop. Instead of twenty tabs and a copy-paste pile, it gives you a single, opinionated surface.

  • Context-aware sessions: keep chat, files, routes, and patches tied to the same workspace instead of starting from scratch every time.
  • Visual flows: sketch logic or UX flows and let AI help with boilerplate, data, or edge-case checking.
  • Ship-ready outputs: the goal is real game scenes, docs, and tools — not just pretty screenshots of UI.

About AtlasForge Interactive

AtlasForge is early, but the intent is simple: build tools we actually use, worlds that feel alive, and an engine that respects both players and operators.

What we’re doing right now

Internally, we’re using Atlas-V concepts and the AtlasStudio workstation to build Octopus in Action and other prototypes.

Externally, we’re in conversation mode: talking to teams who care about simulation, operations, and AI-assisted work — and shaping which pieces of this stack should ship first.

If that sounds like you, we’d rather talk early than over-polish a deck.

Is Atlas-V available today?

Not as a public product. Pieces of the engine are being used internally and hardened against real workloads. The goal is to open it to a small set of partners before anything “fully public.”

Can we use AtlasStudio now?

AtlasStudio is currently an internal workstation. If you’re interested in a future collaborator build, reach out and tell us what your team is trying to do.

Do you only work on games?

No. Games are our favorite way to stress-test ideas, but Atlas-V is deliberately being shaped for hybrid use: simulations, internal tools, and operational flows.

What kind of teams should contact you?

Studios or teams who think in systems, who don’t want a generic chatbot bolted to their product, and who are okay co-designing an engine instead of buying a sealed black box.
