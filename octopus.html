World · Harbor City

Octopus in Action is a coastal city built out of tasks and routes.

The world is centered on a working harbor, sleepy neighborhoods, and the small systems that keep everything moving—deliveries, patrols, markets, and the occasional bit of trouble.

Under the hood, those stories look like structured work: queues, chains, routes, and snapshots that align with the AtlasForge stack.

World identity

Warm orange, harbor lights, and a soft-lined mascot define the OIA look.

Setting

A compact harbor city with dense systems.

The map is small on purpose—every block matters. Each district has its own routes, schedules, and quirks that can be turned into tasks and chains.

Harbor & docks

Cargo, ferries, night-fishing, and market boats. Routes here define supply, schedules, and risk around weather and timing.

Old town

Narrow streets, stacked apartments, and local shops. Patrols, noise complaints, deliveries, and festivals all share the same streets.

Hills & outskirts

Quieter areas with long roads and vantage points. Great for long-range routes, high-ground events, and slow-burn stories.

Loops

The city runs on small, repeatable systems.

Each loop is simple on its own—but layered together, they create a living, reactive city perfect for simulation and management-style gameplay.

Mailbox → Task → Route

Requests appear as letters or signals: deliveries, patrols, inspections, events. They resolve into concrete routes you can track and improve.

Shifts & time blocks

Morning markets, mid-day traffic, evening crowds, and late-night quiet. Time-of-day acts as a natural modifier for risk and reward.

Neighborhood mood

Each district has a mood that responds to recent events: good runs, failures, long waits, or surprise moments.
