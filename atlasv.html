Engine

Atlas-V describes how work moves through the stack.

The engine breaks every flow into four concerns: navigation, memory, guardrails, and simulation. Together they define how features travel from idea to release.

AtlasStudio is the surface. Atlas-V is the model beneath it: the language that explains what should happen to each feature, task, risk, and patch.

Layers

Four core modules, one shared language.

Every project in AtlasForge can be described in the same structure: how you find work, how you remember what happened, how you protect the system, and how you rehearse outcomes.

Navigator

Browsing and routing: which work appears, in what order, under which filters and tags. Ties directly into AtlasStudio’s queues and search patterns.

Memory

Decisions, notes, and version state. Tracks whether a feature was approved, deferred, rejected, or expanded—and why.

Guard

Safety, constraints, and caps. Defines limits, edge cases, and rules that keep flows within acceptable parameters.

FlowSim

Timelines and scenarios. Models when work should land, how long it takes, and what happens under different assumptions.

Active module: Navigator

Navigator · entry & routing

Controls what appears in the user’s field of view: which queue, which filter, and which feature enters the spotlight next.

  • Inputs: queues, tags, search, current world/context.
  • Outputs: an ordered list of candidates for the active panel.
  • AtlasStudio tie-in: left column behavior and quick-jumps.

Memory · decisions & state

Tracks every choice: Yes, No, Later, Expand, plus notes and snapshot references.

  • Inputs: user actions, feature metadata, timestamps.
  • Outputs: state per feature, history, and snapshot anchors.
  • AtlasStudio tie-in: decision buttons and history view.

Guard · constraints & safety

Encodes rules, maximums, and conditions that keep flows safe and coherent.

  • Inputs: thresholds, roles, environment settings.
  • Outputs: allow/deny flags, warnings, and caps.
  • AtlasStudio tie-in: warnings, blocked actions, risk ribbons.

FlowSim · scenarios & timelines

Looks at time and capacity: when features land, how long they stay open, and how routes behave under different assumptions.

  • Inputs: durations, dependencies, sequence constraints.
  • Outputs: projected timelines, stress-test scenarios.
  • AtlasStudio tie-in: schedule views and release strips.
