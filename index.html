The Atlas stack

Three pillars, one mental model.

Every project in AtlasForge uses the same idea: treat your world like a live routing problem between people, tools, and time.

AtlasStudio

A three-panel workstation for shaping content, behavior, and flows. Built to feel like a creative suite, not a config page.

  • Brainstorm → UAT → Review → Patch pipeline
  • Fake builds, real structure
  • Targets games, tools, and docs

Atlas-V Engine

A routing brain that thinks in orbits: navigator, memory, guard, and flow-sim. Designed for any system with decisions and time.

  • Explainable flows, not black boxes
  • Timeline-aware decisions
  • Safe sandboxes before production

Octopus in Action

A cozy harbor-town sim with a gentleman octopus mayor. This is the flagship world we use to prove the stack in practice.

  • Mailbox-driven civic tasks
  • Day/night and schedule rhythms
  • Playable testbed for Atlas-V ideas

What AtlasForge is for

Simulation-first everything.

The same toolkit that powers a harbor-town sim can power your operations dashboards, knowledge tools, and interactive docs. The difference is only in the art and the stakes.

  • Games & sims: management games, city-sims, training, or explorable “what-if” worlds.
  • Ops & tools: internal dashboards, routing logic, incident timelines, and “Atlas-V style” explanations.
  • Knowledge & docs: guided experiences that turn a static knowledge base into a navigable story.

Current focus

Right now AtlasForge is focused on getting the internal stack right: a clean workstation, a trustworthy engine layer, and one flagship game that exercises all of it.

  • AtlasStudio UI & workflow polish
  • Atlas-V orbit & “explain my decision” language
  • OIA: mailbox loop, city hall decisions, harbor life

Who this is for

Small teams, weird projects, long arcs.

AtlasForge is built around a single idea: bring world-design and system-design into one stack. Everything we build — engines, tools, and games — follows that shared philosophy.

Indie & micro-studios

Teams that want systems depth without building a custom engine for every idea. Think “one toolbox, many worlds.”

Ops-heavy teams

People who manage queues, schedules, and policy but think in stories, not just spreadsheets.

Tinkerers & hybrids

Anyone who wants their personal projects, tools, and games to live in the same universe instead of scattered side projects.
